SECURITY REFLECTION

During this security audit, I identified several cybersecurity risks in my own digital habits. Although I use strong passwords for most of my accounts, I discovered that Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) was not enabled on all of them. This increases the risk of unauthorized access if a password is compromised. I also realized that keeping software and operating systems fully updated is important because outdated systems may contain security vulnerabilities that attackers can exploit.

The security assessment helped me understand the importance of regularly reviewing account security settings. I learned that recovery methods such as recovery emails and phone numbers are essential for regaining access to accounts if they are compromised. I also became more aware of common cyber threats such as phishing, malware, ransomware, social engineering, and data breaches.

As an immediate improvement, I plan to enable MFA on all important accounts, including GitHub and LinkedIn. I will also ensure that my operating system and applications are updated regularly. In addition, I will remain cautious when opening emails, clicking links, or downloading files from unknown sources. Using unique passwords for different accounts and storing them securely will also help improve my overall security.

Cybersecurity is important in today's world because people rely heavily on digital systems for communication, banking, education, and business. A single security incident can result in financial loss, privacy violations, and damage to personal or organizational data. Practicing good cyber hygiene and following security best practices can significantly reduce these risks and help create a safer digital environment for everyone.
